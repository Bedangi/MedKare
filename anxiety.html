THE ANXIETY TEST

An anxiety test is a type of psychology-based assessment, designed to measure the severity of anxiety symptoms experienced by an individual. These tests are commonly used in clinical psychology, counselling, and psychiatry to assist in diagnosing anxiety disorders and evaluating treatment outcomes. There are various types of anxiety tests, each focusing on different aspects of anxiety. Here we are doing Sinha's Comprehensive Anxiety Test.


SINHA’S COMPREHENSIVE ANXIETY TEST

Sinha's Comprehensive Anxiety Test (SCAT) is a psychological test designed to assess the severity of anxiety in individuals. It is a widely used tool in both clinical and research settings.

  • What it measures: The SCAT measures generalized anxiety, which is characterized by excessive and persistent worry about a variety of things, as well as specific anxieties, such as social anxiety, phobias, and panic disorder.
  • How it works: The SCAT is a self-report questionnaire that consists of 40 items. Each item describes a symptom of anxiety, and respondents’ rate how much they experience that symptom on a scale of 0 (not at all) to 4 (very much).
  • Scoring: The scores are summed up to provide a total score, which can then be interpreted to determine the severity of the individual's anxiety.
  • Administration: The SCAT can be administered individually or in a group setting. It typically takes about 15-20 minutes to complete.
